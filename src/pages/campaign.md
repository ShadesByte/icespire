# Campaign Summary

_A living reference for Owen's campaign. Last updated: Session 5 (7/14/26)._

**Setting:** Forgotten Realms, Phandalin region — *Dragon of Icespire Peak*,
D&D 5e (2014 rules), level cap 6.
**House rules:** no Neverwinter location; no tracking of ammunition or weight
except special ammunition or absurd loads.

## The story so far

### Sessions 0–1 — The Road to Phandalin (4/27/26)

The party formed on the road and entered Phandalin, where they worked the
quest board, met the Quest Master, and picked up the **Gnomengarde Quest**:
warn the reclusive gnome tinkerers about a dragon, with a bonus for recovering
magic items. Dragon rumors first surfaced here. A strange figure watched them
at the inn — a thread that pays off later. Camping en route, they killed a
wild boar — Barnaby's Dragon's Breath and Sage's reluctant finishing arrow —
and preserved it for food.

### Session 2 — Dax Joins (5/15/26)

The party found **Dax** wounded amid five dead orcs, on a parallel quest to
Gnomengarde and carrying orc rumors of an area dragon. He cautiously joined.
They explored Gnomengarde, met gnome mushroom-farmers, and a brusque baker who
pointed Dax toward the king. Then **Facktoré**, an unhinged gnome, shot Thom
with a crossbow ballista; Thom slept her, Dax tied her up, and Bean pocketed
her 10 gold. Rut later freed her, and she fled screeching.

### Session 3 — The Mimics (5/18/26)

The party worked through the throne room (hidden passage, peephole into the
king's chambers, a bound queen, a paranoid king checking everything for
mimics), disabled traps, crossed a goop-covered rope bridge, and beat **two
mimics** in a storage room. The **Guildmaster's contract was delivered to the
King**, and Dax received a **sealed envelope from the King** — proof of the
completed trade.

### Session 4 — Back to Phandalin, and a Squeeze (6/22/26)

Harbin Wester paid 135 gold (19 each), returned the wand, and offered two
contracts: **Umbrage Hill** (get midwife Adabra Gwynn, a Colletite of
Chauntea, to leave her windmill, 25 gp) and the **Dwarven Excavation** (warn
dwarf prospectors about a white dragon, 50 gp). At the Miner's Exchange, Dax
delivered the King's sealed letter to **Guildmaster Holia Thornton**; the
letter's dragon mention prompted her questions. She then revealed a
**Zhentarim** dragon-pin (recognized by Hamish), leveled crossbows at the
party, and forced an intel-sharing pact, paying for dragon information. The
guard posted outside — one of her Zhentarim people — was the same strange
figure who'd been watching the party at the inn back in Session 1. With his
original contract discharged, Dax stayed pragmatic — happy to keep working
with Thornton so long as coin was on the table. Thornton also flagged a ruined
temple south along the **Trivial Trail** where locals stash gold. Bean lifted
Harbin's private notes on Thornton during an unauthorized return visit (Dax
and Rut sat it out). At Umbrage Hill, the party fought off **two manticores**
(Dax burned an Action Surge; Barnaby shielded him with a reaction; Bean and
Sage landed the kills). The windmill mostly survived.

### Session 5 — Adabra, Orcs, and the Temple of Abbathor (7/14/26)

Post-manticore, Rut's Goodberry and Barnaby's Lay on Hands stabilized the
party; Rut ripped a tail-spike from a manticore corpse and kept it — why,
he hasn't said. **Adabra
Gwynn** refused to relocate (she can only brew the town's healing potions at
her windmill), gave a free healing potion (Dax tossed it to a bleeding Thom),
noted rising orc activity, and sent a note back to Harbin. En route to the
excavation, the party found day-old orc tracks and confirmed a **large orc
encampment** down a split trail (bypassed). At the excavation — a ruined
settlement behind a broken black-stone gate — they met dwarf prospectors
**Norbus Ithermel** and **Dazlyn Grayshard**, delivered the dragon warning,
and negotiated a **60/40 treasure split** in the party's favor (Thom) plus
lodging. Dax's Dwarvish introduction earned goodwill. The site is a ruined
**temple of Abbathor** (god of greed): displeased with his priests, the god
buried the settlement in an avalanche and transformed the survivors into the
**ochre jellies** now blocking the dig. In the morning fight the party learned
that slashing splits jellies (Dax's longsword follow-up backfired after his
thrown handaxe already split one). Notable beats: Thom's Magic Missile
triggered a Wild Magic Surge that aged him years; Bean's natural-20 sneak
attack; kills spread across the group. Loot: a hidden **coffer of gemstones**
(Dax found it, went to Thom, dwarves unaware), and an acid-etched **gold
necklace** overwritten in ancient Dwarvish — *"This is what happens to those
who displease me"* — that Rut pocketed. Two secret doors opened: one to a
glinting rubble hallway, one to a closed door. The session ended with Hamish
investigating the glint — **another ochre jelly** lurking in the rubble.
