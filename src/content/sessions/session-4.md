After wrapping up business at Gnomengarde, the party headed back to Phandalin
to collect their reward and figure out next steps.

First stop: Harbin Wester's house. After a comical series of unlocking sounds,
the nervous, portly townmaster cracked open the door, scanned the sky, and
hurried the party inside. He paid out 135 gold total (19 each) for the
Gnomengarde job, returned the wand, and offered two new contracts:

- **Umbrage Hill** — convince the midwife Adabra Gwynn, a Colletite of
  Chauntea, to leave her stone windmill and return to town for safety (25 gp).
- **Dwarven Excavation** — warn dwarf prospectors southwest of town that a
  white dragon has moved into the area (50 gp).

Next, the Miner's Exchange. Dax delivered the King's sealed letter to
**Guildmaster Holia Thornton** — proof of the completed trade — and the
letter's mention of the dragon prompted her questions about the threat. Then
she drew back her cloak to reveal a pin bearing a dragon symbol, which Hamish
recognized as the mark of the **Zhentarim**, a shadow organization with
fingers in organized crime and politics. The guard posted outside — the same
strange figure who'd watched the party at the inn back in Session 1 — was one
of her people. What started as a conversation turned into a strong-arm:
Thornton had crossbows trained on the party and pressed everyone into an
information-sharing agreement, demanding to be kept informed of any dragon
intelligence and offering payment for updates. With his original contract
discharged, Dax stayed pragmatic — happy to keep working with her so long as
coin stayed on the table. Thornton also let slip a lead: a ruined temple south
along the **Trivial Trail**, where locals stash their gold.

While most of the party went shopping, a few members made an unauthorized
visit back to Harbin's place. Bean turned up some of his personal notes on
Thornton. Dax and Rut sat this one out.

The party then set out for Umbrage Hill — and found **two manticores** actively
trying to tear the stone windmill apart. A long, scrappy fight followed: fog
cloud, magic missiles, handaxes, sneak attacks, and an Action Surge from Dax,
with Barnaby burning a reaction to shield Dax mid-fight. Bean landed the kill
on the first manticore, and Sage put an arrow through the second one's eye to
close it out.

Adabra's windmill survives. Mostly.
