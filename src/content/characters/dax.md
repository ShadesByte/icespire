The party found Dax in Session 2, wounded amid five dead orcs on a parallel
contract to Gnomengarde. He joined cautiously — unsure at first whether the
group was the fastest way to finish his quest — and stayed because the jobs
keep paying.

With his original trade contract discharged (the Guildmaster's contract
delivered to King Korboz, the King's sealed envelope delivered back to
Guildmaster Thornton), Dax works wherever the coin is: Harbin's contracts, and
Thornton's standing offer of payment for dragon intelligence. He's the party's
contractual link to the Zhentarim guildmaster — though he sat out Bean's
unauthorized visit to Harbin's house, and doesn't know what her notes say.

## Background

Dax grew up poor in a mid-sized town — the kind of place where opportunity was
scarce and survival took precedence over ambition. With his family struggling
to get by, he discovered early that he had a natural talent for fighting and
put it to use in the local fighting rings. Not for glory; for coin. It worked.
For years he fought, and for years his family got by.

When they passed, there was nothing left tying him to that town, so he left.
With little else to his name and a knack for fighting, he enlisted in the
military. It wasn't a calling — it was the next logical step. The military
took his raw ability and sharpened it: proper swordwork, discipline, how to
fight as part of a unit and how to survive alone. He did his time, did his job
well, and when his service was up he walked away without looking back.

Civilian life meant finding his own work, and sellsword suited him — no fixed
employer, no fixed home, just the next job. His military background meant
people hired him with confidence, and he built a quiet reputation for being
reliable and straightforward. Clients hire someone they trust, and they hire
them again.

He'd been working the roads around Phandalin when the Guildmaster took notice
and offered him a one-time contract: travel to Gnomengarde and establish trade
relations with the gnomes on behalf of the guild. Simple enough on paper. The
roving orc bands in the area made it anything but.

Fighting through those bands, something caught his attention. The orcs weren't
just aggressive — they were unsettled, agitated in a way that went beyond
territory disputes. In the chaos of skirmishes he picked up enough to piece
together that something had them scared. Something they called a *dragon*. He
doesn't know what to make of it. But orcs don't scare easily, and that's
enough to take seriously.

## Appearance

Human, 25, 5'11" and 175 lb. Short brown hair, slightly longer on top and
intentionally shaped; blue eyes, fair skin, light scruff along the jaw. Small
scattered scars mark his knuckles and forearms from years of fighting in the
ring — nothing dramatic, just lived-in. He carries himself with quiet,
understated confidence.

## Personality

He faces problems head-on: a simple, direct solution is the best path to
success. Always polite and respectful with it. He fights for those who cannot
fight for themselves, but holds that ideals aren't worth killing over or going
to war for — live and let live. His one confessed flaw: he'd rather eat his
armor than admit when he's wrong (see: "Wasn't in the military very long
anyway").
